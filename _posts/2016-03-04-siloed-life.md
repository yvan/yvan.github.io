silo patterns:

i have remarked a curious trend among humans living in rich western countries and especially rich cities like new york. i call it the silo pattern.

the silo pattern is a behavior intended to protect the emotion health of the human by separating the components of human life into different categories. this concept is rooted firmly in our culture; we are all creating silos, not thinking much of this status quo. for example:

'dating'
'celebrity'
'my public image'
'work life balance'

are all examples of activities or concepts that rely on silo patterns.

at its core a silo pattern is the act of separating life into different components, creating different personalities that are optimized for each of the silos.

why silo patterns are bad:

silo patterns are fundamentally anti-social and destroy intimacy. because we have accepted silo patterns we seek the idea of intimacy. those that actually seek intimacy feel so alien relative to other humans they ask themselves whther they are human at all.

let's look at a tangible example:
```
human karl has a partner or girlfriend.

human karl makes a silo for his love life and a silo for his work.

he does not let his partner help him with work.

he does not let his work contribute anything to his love life.

he has two silos.
```

the effect of these silos is that if karl's girlfriend breaks up with him or if his work becomes demanding. either silo (the love life or his work) can die and the other silo will be unaffected. this is why a human might want to employ silo patterns. it protects some aspect of their life.

by employing silo patterns the human decomposes her life and ends up with fragments. these fragments amount to a scattered and confused existence. if you see humans walking around new york, just look long enough, eventually you will see someone with is alive but inside seems dead. although they seem to inhabit human bodies it is likely that this person has become a colllection of silos. with enough self reflcetion this deconstruction can be reversed. most do not slf reflect and remain fragmented.the end goal is not to have a set of working silos, a fragmented spirit. the end goal is to live a good human life. and whatever that means to any human in particular following silo patterns will only preserve the silos. it will not preserve the human.

these silos do nothing to protect the human. they protect only themselves. if a human sets up a work silo and a love silo and the love silo breaks. the human will still be feel pain. the only thing these silos do is create a false sense of security, a delusion for humans to live inside. when this delusion inevitably breaks the results are often more damaging than a realistic unsiloed life.

conclusion:

humans can keep the silos or parts of their lives working but no matter how hard they try they cannot stop themselves from getting hurt.

you can silo your life.

you should not.
