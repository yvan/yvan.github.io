why nations fail - daron acemoglu, james a. robinson : the core message of the book focusing on institutional factors of prosperity good, if a bit repretitive. i found the author's unwillingness to link culture's role to institutional outcomes a bit frustrating. :: 4/5

assasin's apprentice - robin hobb : a really great fantasy novel, very good to escape the laments of this world if only for a little while. :: 4/5

hackers & painters - paul graham : a great, sometimes rambling book on what happened in silicon valley and its internal cultural thought in the 90s and early 00s. :: 3/5

how google works - eric schmidt, jonathan rosenberg : a nice bit of propaganda from google, some useful tidbits sprinkled in on how google managed issues early on and scaled up their organization. :: 2/5

medidations - marcus aurelius : a great book if you want to understand the stoic mindset, the book, like stoicism can be bland at times. :: 4/5

history of money - mike thornton : probably a bit too long but appropriately thorough nonetheless. i liked the chapters discussing modern digital currencies like bitcoin. :: 3/5

elon musk - ashlee vance : a nice read about the life and missions of elon musk. he seems important these days it might be wise to check in on how the guy thinks a little bit. pay special attention to the all the appendices elon added himself, pretty interesting. :: 3/5

programming from the ground up - jonathan bartlett : really loved this book, it teaches assembly as an intriductory language which should be done more often. it was great practice to touch up on my x86 and the simplicity of each step in assembly programming really clears the mind. :: 4/5

dune messiah - frank herbert : not as good as dune and the whole thing kind of has a weird "inevitable doom is on the horizon" message but it was enjoyable to be immersed in the world a second time. :: 4/5

mein kampf - adolf hitler : the book explains how hitler came to develop his antisemetic, anticommunist views as well as his ideas for "german democracy." (it's just facism). a lot of his "evidence" for supproting anti-semitism is inaccurate and could be described as conspiracy theory. the book is a helpful guide for the future; hopefully by reading this people can be exposed to and understand how bad this man's ideas really were. :: unrated

infantry attacks - erwin rommel : i really enjoyed the bragadocious descriptions of rommel's time as an infantry captain in ww1. erwin's brushes with death reminded me of the tenuous nature of life and how it often hangs by a thread. it recalled analogus from my own close experiences with mortality. sometimes it's a little dry. :: 4/5

high growth handbook - elad gil : it's a nice guide if you have a growing startup on which patterns seem to work in silicon valley. :: 3/5

the martian chronicles - ray bradbury : these are nice retro sci-fi stories about a small outgrowth of human civilization on mars that observes the rest humanity destroys itself. i particularly liked the native martians and themese explored in their rare but continued presence on mars. :: 4/5

the wizard of menlo park - randall e. stross : nice autobiography of thomas edison and his strengths/weaknesses. :: 4/5

48 laws of power - robert greene : interesting take on ways in which power can be applied and historical examples for the successful and unsuccessful application. i particularly liked examples of failure from figures that in prior chapters succeeded. :: 4/5

children of time - adrian tchaikovsky : really nice and sometimes surprising twists in a clash of civilzations. :: 4/5

the three body problem - cixin liu : it was a totally new experience in sci-fi for me. read this book. :: 5/5

the dark forest - cixin liu : great follow up to the three body problem. fascinating ideas about how civilizations might compete in a universe of limited resources. the translator was different and i think that affected the way ideas were communicated in teh book. :: 4/5

death's end - cixin liu : good ideas yet again. i didn't love the way the story went but deeply appreciate the conclusion to this wonderful piece of art. :: 4/5
